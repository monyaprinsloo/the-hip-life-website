WHO AM I?

About Me

Hi there! I’m a Cape Town-based 4th-year medical student by day and a wannabe runner by… well, whenever I can fit it in between studying and life.

With a passion for holistic health, I strive to balance the mental, physical, and emotional chaos of life — think of it as my personal triathlon, but with fewer medals and more snacks. On a more serious note, I’m all about raising awareness for hip-related issues (because having a hip that’s not actually hip is surprisingly common). My journey through surgeries and recovery inspired me to share my experiences and promote well-being in every aspect—mind, body, and soul.

About @the.hip.life

What started as a tiny Instagram account with just a handful of followers, quickly turned into something special. I began by documenting my recovery after my first surgeries—partly to process my own journey, and partly because I would’ve loved to know what to expect.

Fast forward a bit, and now @the.hip.life is home to a vibrant, diverse group of wonderful people affectionately known as "hippys."

It’s become a safe space where we laugh, learn, and occasionally whine about physiotherapy together. I genuinely love interacting with this incredible community—it’s like having an extended family, but with fewer awkward reunions and more support.

My surgeries

  • Left FO: November 2021
  • Left labrum reconstruction, capsular tightening, psoas release: January 2025

My hope for "hippys"

Vision: To build a supportive, fun, and empowering community where hip issues are less of a burden and more of a shared experience that connects people across the world.

Mission: To provide honest, relatable content about hip surgeries and recovery, foster positivity in the face of challenges, and spread a little humor—because laughter might not be the best medicine, but it’s definitely cheaper than therapy.
